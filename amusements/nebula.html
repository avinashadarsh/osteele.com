Nebula

[Reload this page with Java installed to see the live exhibit.]

Nebula is a suggestion of swirling clouds of self-illuminated gas. It simulates the mixture of multiple layers of paint combined with video feedback around multiple points of symmetry dispersed in space.

Nebula is based on Frost and Fire, a screen saver I wrote for the Macintosh in 1989. Frost and Fire used a bitmap rotation algorithm I had invented when I heard someone had done uncached microcomputer bitmap rotation at interactive rates. ("Someone" was Live Picture, and the report was wrong — they used a precomputed cache.) Ten years later, interactive bitmap rotation doesn't take any special tricks: incrementally compiled floating point Java is faster than hand-coded fixed-point assembly was a decade ago.

Oliver Steele
