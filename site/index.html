Content Creation

We're four years* into the twenty-first century and still nothing has caught up with Emacs as a tool for automating the repetition out of editing and program workflow. I'm hoping for something that makes more effective use of bitmap displays in the next millenium, though.

James Clark's nxml-mode makes Emacs more palatable. It's why I switched from XEmacs, whose "batteries included" philosophy had previously won me over to it.

OmniGraffle is the perfect drawing program for someone who can't draw. When I need to render a diagram, it makes it look like I knew what I was doing. Like many Mac programs, good design choices are baked into the program. Please tell me if there's anything like this for the PC!

Publishing

WordPress is responsible for compositing the pages. I cut my blogging teeth on MovableType and wouldn't even have minded paying for it again, but got tired of the page rebuilds and excited by the pace of WP development after the MT exodus. (I used to use a variety of custom solutions, starting with some homebrew Lisp code in 1995.)

WordPress is built with PHP and MySQL. I like Python better than PHP, and my brother in law keeps telling me that PostgreSQL is better than MySQL, but I'd rather use a well-crafted solution assembled from suboptimal pieces than build this particular beast myself.

The PyWordNet docs are an experiment in using DocBook, ant, and some custom XSLT to build a site.

Hosting

DreamHost does my hosting. They don't run Java (I can understand why) or X11; other than that, I'm very happy with them.

Staging

I use a Dell Latitude running Windows XP and Apache as my editing station and staging server. I'd like to switch back to a Macintosh, but I really like the sub-four-pound form factor that you can get with a PC.

Design

I'm not a designer (no snide comments, please!), but I enjoy trying to learn enough about design and design tools that I can appreciate what designers do. I've drawn inspiration for this site “design” from the default WordPress distribution by Dave Shea and Matthew Mullenweg, from Stopdesign by Douglas Bowman, and from Alex King, most recently. Graphic design glitches are purely mine, and are because (1) I'm still learning CSS and (2) my eye isn't that good anyway, and will be slowly addressed over the next decade.

* Or three years, depending on how geeky you are about when the century started.
